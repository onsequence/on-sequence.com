halt and catch fire

Serial television series that are set in the past better reflect the era in which the work was created versus the era depicted. Mad Men tells us more about a post 9/11, post-financial crisis America than it does about the 1960s. Likewise, Halt and Catch Fire documents America's second tech-boom through the lens of it's first. In exploring the plight of early computation, it opens our imaginations to the possibilities of the future.
