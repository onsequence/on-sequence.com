halt and catch fire

Time pieces are more indicative of the era in which a work is created, versus an accurate representation of the period in question. Mad Men tells us more about a post 9/11, post-financial crisis America than it does about the idyllic sixties. Likewise, Halt and Catch Fire documents America's second tech-boom through the lens of the first. In exploring the plight of early computation, it opens our imaginations to the possibilities of the future.
